A. p. J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

"Don't take rest after your first victory because if you fail in second, more lips are waiting to say that your first victory was just luck"

Biography

APJ Abdul Kalam, also known as Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist, engineer, and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and died on July 27, 2015. Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to the development of India's missile technology. He played a key role in the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), India's first satellite launch vehicle, and was also instrumental in the development of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Prior to his political career, Kalam served as a scientist and engineer at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was also a professor of aerospace engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Chennai. Kalam was widely respected for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to public service. He was a recipient of numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. He authored several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds". Kalam was a popular and beloved figure in India, and his death was mourned by people across the country. He remains an inspiration to many for his contributions to science, technology, and public service.
